How to Register Your SIM Card in the Philippines

With the implementation of the SIM Registration Act (Republic Act No. 11934), all Filipinos are required to register their SIM cards. This is a significant step towards curbing fraud and enhancing mobile security. Whether you have a Globe, Smart, Sun, TNT, Dito, or GoMo SIM card, this guide will help you through the registration process.

Step-by-Step Guide to SIM Registration

1. Prepare Your Information

Before starting the registration process, gather the necessary information:

  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Address
  • Valid identification (ID)
  • Your mobile number

2. Visit the Official Registration Website

To register your new SIM, visit the official registration site simregistrations.org. This site is trusted by Filipinos, as well as foreign visitors and tourists, and provides the latest and correct information on the SIM registration process for your specific telecom provider, including,

  • Globe SIM Registration
  • TM SIM Registration
  • Smart SIM Registration
  • Sun SIM Registration
  • TNT SIM Registration
  • Dito SIM Registration
  • GoMo SIM Registration

3. Fill Out the Registration Form

Complete the online registration form with the required details. Ensure that all information is accurate to avoid issues later on.

4. Submit Your Registration

Once you’ve filled out the form, submit it and wait for a confirmation message. This may take a few minutes, so be patient.

5. Keep Your Confirmation

After successful registration, you’ll receive a confirmation message. Save this for your records as it may be required in the future.

Important

Registering your SIM card is crucial for compliance with the new SIM law, also known as the Republic Act No. 11934. This is for protecting your mobile identity theft, avoid spam messages, and other cyber crimes. Make sure to register your SIM before deadline to avoid any disruptions.
